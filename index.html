The idea

A thinking partner, not an answer machine.

Most tools race to hand you an answer. Scaffold does the opposite — it asks the questions that make your thinking sharper.

It asks, it doesn't answer

Scaffold guides you with questions instead of conclusions. The insight is always yours.

Your closed universe

It draws only from the context you bring — it doesn't pull in outside sources, so the reasoning stays yours to own.

You decide when you're done

Scaffold offers to wrap up when it senses you've addressed your inquiry — but concluding is always your call.

The method

A Socratic questioning approach.

Through Socratic questioning, Scaffold pushes you to deeply engage with a topic of your choosing. Rather than filling in the blanks for you, it helps you do the harder, more valuable work of thinking it through yourself. Specifically, it helps you:

Surface your underlying assumptions · Consider the internal consistency of your beliefs · Explore the implications of your thinking.

Importantly, Scaffold draws from the closed system that is your universe. It does not reference outside sources, and is therefore unable to verify the accuracy of your statements. You are responsible for compiling the relevant context — Scaffold will guide you in parsing through it. For factual answers, consult additional resources.

How it goes

It takes just one thought to start.

Bring a thought

Start with anything you want to think through — an opinion on the news, an argument for an essay, a perspective on what it means to lead a good life.

Get questioned

Scaffold responds not with answers, but with questions that probe your reasoning, test your assumptions, and open up angles you hadn't considered.

Think it through

Capture what surfaces along the way with sticky notes, and follow the thread wherever it leads. The work — and the payoff — is yours.

Conclude, and keep it

When you're satisfied, wrap up the interaction, distill your takeaways, and save or export it to revisit later.

So, what do you want to think about?

One thought is all it takes to begin.
